Why CTB

International trade does not always need a new currency. It needs better clearing.

A cross-border trade appears to require a standalone payment. Across recurring obligations among several firms, however, some flows offset one another and need not be funded or transferred in full.

Gross obligations: 300. Each participant's net position: 0. Real amounts and maturities rarely align perfectly. A historical replay measures how much liquidity and operational movement can be removed safely after legal and risk constraints.

CTB checks whether obligations are eligible and legally compatible, computes each participant's net position and then creates residual settlement instructions. This can reduce peak liquidity, transaction count and operational failure points.

CTB is not “digital barter”, a new reserve currency or a way to override applicable law. It is a regulated pre-clearing layer above permitted settlement rails: eligibility, multilateral netting, risk limits, commodity title and a common evidence trail.

01 · Corrected instrument model

Four explicit objects — not one ambiguous “commodity token”

CBU

Valuation unit

Normalises prices, limits and netting. It is not money, title or a promise of future goods.

PSB

Prefunded balance

A segregated bank or escrow balance used during a short settlement cycle.

ADC

Delivery claim

Issued only after control over an identified commodity lot has been verified.

ETR

Title record

An electronic bill of lading or warehouse record where applicable law recognises it.

The essential v4 correction: future production and productive capacity are not represented as present collateral. Before verified goods or a valid ETR exist, the system records an obligation against escrow — it does not issue an ADC.

02 · Controlled risk

Losses are allocated — not hidden in a universal fund

Margin reflects volatility, liquidation horizon, basis, freight, quality and concentration. Hard debit and tenor caps prevent a surplus participant from becoming an involuntary long-term creditor.

03 · Evidence before infrastructure

Replay the obligations first. Move money later.

Gate 0

Demand

Anonymised historical obligations, at least three anchor participants and willingness to pay.

Gate 1

Law

Finality, netting, insolvency, title, licensing, tax and data opinions.

Gate 2

Risk

Calibrated margin, prefunding, limits, waterfall and committed resources.

Gate 3

Live

Shadow mode, capped live pilot and independent review before expansion.

India–UAE and other BRICS-linked commodity corridors are candidates, not conclusions. The corridor is selected by measurable netting potential, committed participants, legal feasibility, title evidence, settlement exits and risk appetite.

04 · Fits existing rails

A pre-clearing layer, not another payment network

With CIPS: CTB can reduce gross obligations before residual settlement in RMB or HKD through CIPS.

With banks or CBDC: the rail moves the residual value; CTB supplies eligibility, netting, limits and evidence.

With regulators: selective disclosure protects commercial data while preserving lawful supervisory and audit access.

Proposed first engagement

A 30-minute technical scoping call

The immediate proposal is deliberately small: identify one recurring trade corridor, replay anonymised historical obligations and decide whether the measured netting and liquidity benefit justifies legal design and a shadow pilot.
