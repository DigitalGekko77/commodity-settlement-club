How to explain CSC in one paragraph

CSC is a commodity-linked settlement perimeter where the digital instrument is tied to legal title and a specific trade contract rather than open-ended synthetic issuance. The point is to reduce intermediation fragility, keep the instrument inside a governed loop, and give public institutions more visibility rather than less.

simple example

A simple example

Scenario

An exporter places oil into a controlled perimeter. An operator issues a digital title linked to that specific commodity lot. The importer settles through a bounded contract-linked mechanism instead of a loosely controlled intermediary route.

What matters

The instrument is not supposed to start circulating as a general-purpose FX-like token. It remains tied to delivery logic and contract enforcement.

faq

FAQ

Is this a new currency?

No. The model does not require immediate launch of a universal BRICS currency. It is framed as a settlement infrastructure for a specific commodity corridor.

How is this different from a gold standard?

CSC avoids a single-anchor design. That reduces monolithic price dependence, single-point vulnerability, and the legal weakness of a “gold token” without direct commodity-title logic.

How is double-pledge risk addressed?

Through perfected control, a separate controlled perimeter, pre-issuance legal verification, and title checks before issuance rather than after the crisis starts.

How does the system avoid becoming a cheap FX bridge?

Through closed-loop settlement. The instrument exists inside a specific contract and is not meant to become a freely circulating currency proxy.

What about persistent corridor imbalance?

The design needs hard debit caps, prefunded liquidity buffers, and reset windows. Without that, any such system risks reproducing the failure mode of older clearing arrangements.

Why would state institutions care?

Because the model may increase structured visibility: central banks gain clearer risk oversight, customs gain better cargo-document-settlement linkage, tax authorities gain cleaner transaction structure, and finance ministries gain a more legible trade-linked fiscal surface.

Why is there not just one PDF brochure?

Because a single brochure is useful only for first contact. Here the site offers three levels instead: short landing page, structured whitepaper, and full explanation with FAQ, plus the complete knowledge-base archive.
